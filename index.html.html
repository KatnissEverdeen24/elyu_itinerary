6:30 AM · Day 1
Baluarte Watchtower
Luna, La Union
★★★★½ (2,136 reviews)

One of La Union's most historically significant landmarks — a 400-year-old watchtower built from coral blocks, adobe, and bricks bound together with lime and egg whites. Originally constructed by pre-Hispanic inhabitants and later used by the Spanish colonial forces as a lookout against pirates, it also served as a US Army communication post during WWII. The circular tower stands about 6–7 meters high and offers sweeping coastal views, especially beautiful at sunrise.

Entrance Fee
₱20 env. fee
Hours
Open 24 hours
Best For
History, Photos
Time Needed
30–45 mins
💡 Tips
  • The ₱20 environmental fee may cover other La Union attractions — keep your ticket
  • Go early (you're already there at sunrise!) to avoid crowds and local photo-guide pressure
  • The pebble beach is just nearby — great for early morning walk after the tower
  • A small steak restaurant is in the area if you need a coffee/snack
7:30 AM · Day 1
Bahay na Bato & Pebble Beach
Luna, La Union
★★★★½ (307 reviews)

Bahay na Bato (House of Stone) is a charming cultural attraction built from the very pebbles of its beach. Inside you'll find different art galleries, sculptures, and antique Filipino architecture. The beach in front is unique — covered in smooth river pebbles instead of sand, creating a soothing, almost therapeutic crunch underfoot. The sound of the waves rolling over pebbles is absolutely serene in the early morning.

Entrance Fee
Covered by ₱20 env. fee
Hours
6:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Best For
Art, Culture, Photos
Time Needed
45–60 mins
💡 Tips
  • Wear comfortable shoes — pebbles can be tricky on bare feet
  • There's a small café/store inside for refreshments and souvenirs
  • The "bahay kubo" architecture is perfect for morning golden-hour photos
  • Arrive early to enjoy the quiet before other tourists arrive
9:30 AM · Day 1
Almeda Man-Made Forest
Balaoan, La Union
★★★★½ (273 reviews)

A picturesque stretch of towering mahogany trees lining the Bacnotan–Luna–Balaoan Road, creating a natural canopy tunnel. While not a developed park, the forest creates a genuinely magical, cool, and shaded atmosphere. It's a beloved stopover for road trippers heading to or from La Union — perfect for quick photos and stretching your legs after a long drive.

Entrance Fee
FREE
Hours
Open 24 hours
Best For

💡 Tips

  • Best angle: park safely on the shoulder and shoot looking down the road
  • Morning light filters beautifully through the canopy — great for nature shots
  • Bring insect repellent if you plan to linger longer
10:00 AM · Day 1 (Brunch)
Kabsat Restaurant
San Juan, La Union
★★★★ (2,544 reviews)

Kabsat is a beloved beachfront Filipino restaurant in San Juan with a relaxed coastal vibe, great natural lighting, and big fans to keep you cool. The menu blends local and modern dishes, with fast and friendly service that impresses even during peak hours. A must-try when in La Union — arrive early to beat the crowds, especially on weekends.

The Mansion Estates
Talogtog, San Juan, La Union
★★★★½ (66 reviews)

A stunning hilltop venue perched above San Juan with breathtaking panoramic views of the coastline and mountains. The Mansion is best experienced during sunset — enjoy coffee or cocktails while watching the sun dip into the West Philippine Sea. The road up is steep and challenging (3 very steep sections), so ensure your vehicle is in good condition. Well worth the effort.

7:00 – 11:00 AM · Day 2
Surfing at Urbiztondo + Explore San Juan
Urbiztondo, San Juan, La Union
Urbiztondo Beach ★★★★½ · El Union ★★★★½

Urbiztondo Beach is La Union's legendary surf spot — the surfing capital of Northern Philippines. Waves here are ideal for beginners (1–3 feet on calm days, up to 5–6 feet when active). After your lesson, check out the Mebuyan Vessel art installation on the beach, then grab La Union's most famous coffee at El Union Coffee before wrapping up the beach morning.

Surf Lesson
₱350–500/hr incl. board
Board Rental
₱100–150/hr
El Union Hours
7:00 AM – 10:00 PM (Sat)
Mebuyan Entry
FREE (view from outside)
💡 Surfing Tips
  • Book a surf instructor directly on the beach — rates are fixed and fair
  • May has good surf conditions — waves are consistent but not too intimidating for beginners
  • 2-hour lesson is the sweet spot for first-timers — enough to get standing up
  • Note: Mebuyan Vessel was damaged by a typhoon in Nov 2025 — it may still be closed; you can view it from outside the caution tape area
  • El Union is iconic — must-try: the Dirty Horchata (cinnamon rice milk with espresso shot)
  • El Union also serves excellent grilled cheese — perfect post-surf snack
⭐ Must-Try at El Union

The Dirty Horchata — a creamy, cinnamon-spiced rice milk drink topped with a shot of espresso. It's a La Union landmark in a cup. Also grab the grilled cheese: buttery, crispy edges, perfectly melted inside.

2:00 – 5:00 PM · Day 2
Agoo Eco Park
Sta. Rita, Agoo, La Union
★★★★½ (608 reviews)

Agoo Eco Park is a stunning man-made agoho (Australian pine) forest nestled right beside the sea — one of La Union's most serene hidden gems. Long rows of trees create natural corridors perfect for picnics and lazy afternoon walks. The park sits right at the coast, giving you forest shade during the afternoon heat and then a clear sightline to the sea for golden hour. The sunset here is genuinely magical.

Entrance Fee
₱20 per person
Hours
Open 24 hours
Facilities
Restrooms, Parking
Best Time
4:30–5:30 PM sunset
💡 Tips
  • Bring a picnic blanket or mat — the ground under the trees is perfect for lounging
  • Pack snacks and drinks — food stalls may be limited inside
  • The beach side of the park is where you get the best sunset views
  • Plenty of parking space available — arrive by 2 PM for the shade spots
  • Bikes and hammocks are sometimes available for rent on site
⭐ Perfect For

Families, barkada picnics, and anyone wanting a peaceful wind-down before the long drive home. The combination of forest, breeze, and sunset is genuinely one of La Union's best-kept secrets.
